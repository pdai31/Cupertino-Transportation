About the Project

The YPSI Transportation Safety & Hazard Reporting Initiative — building safer streets through community reporting, data analysis, and public education.

Why Transportation Safety Matters

Every day, students, families, cyclists, and pedestrians rely on Cupertino's roads and paths. Yet many critical safety issues are never documented — residents don't know where to send complaints, aren't sure if others share the same problem, or lack a way to track recurring issues. Faded crosswalks, unsafe bike lanes, and frequent "near-miss" intersections go unaddressed because community observations go uncollected. This project bridges that gap.

Project Goals

🌐

Crowdsourced Reporting

A public website where residents categorize safety concerns, pin exact locations on a map, and upload photos.

📈

Data Analysis

Analyze reports for trends and clusters, producing a Transportation Safety Report with recommendations for the city.

📢

Public Awareness

Infographics, social media safety tips, and presentations promoting safe walking and cycling habits.

🚴

BikeFest Scavenger Hunt

An interactive educational event teaching participants to identify safe bike infrastructure.

🤝

Community Outreach

Surveys, platform promotion, and presenting findings to city transportation staff and stakeholders.

🎓

Student Learning

Real-world experience in software development, data analysis, civic engagement, and project management.
