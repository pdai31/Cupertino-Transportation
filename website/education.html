🎓 Transportation Safety Education

Practical guides for biking, walking, and scooting safely around Cupertino — for students, parents, and everyone in between.

Safety Guides

🚴

Bike Safety

  • Ride with traffic, never against it
  • Use bike lanes where available
  • Make eye contact with drivers at intersections
  • Use lights at night — white front, red rear
  • E-bikes: know your class (1 & 2 allowed on most paths)
🚶

Walking Safety

  • Cross only at crosswalks and signals
  • Look left–right–left before crossing
  • Stay visible: bright clothing at dusk and dawn
  • Put phones away while crossing
  • Watch for turning cars — even with a walk signal
🛴

Scooter & E-Bike Safety

  • Helmets required under 18 — always recommended
  • One rider per scooter, always
  • Ride at safe speeds near pedestrians
  • Know local e-bike rules and licensing requirements
  • Get training before riding an e-bike to school

🪖 Helmet Fit Guide

A helmet only works if it fits. Use the 2-V-1 rule:

  • 2: Two finger-widths between eyebrows and helmet edge
  • V: Straps form a "V" just under each ear
  • 1: One finger fits between chin and strap — no more

Replace any helmet after a crash, even if it looks fine.

✋ Hand Signals Every Rider Should Know

👈Left turn — left arm straight out
☝️Right turn — left arm bent up (or right arm straight out)
👇Stop / slowing — left arm bent down, palm back

Signal early — at least 100 feet before turning.

🏫 Safe Routes to School

Cupertino Safe Routes to School is the city's largest youth-focused transportation program, working on safer bike lanes, pedestrian improvements, reduced school traffic, and e-bike safety education. High school students can join the SR2S Working Group alongside city staff, schools, and parents — and have real influence on transportation planning.
